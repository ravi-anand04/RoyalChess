Rules of the game

Basic Moves

Chess is a two-player strategy board game. The game is played on an 8x8 grid known as a chessboard. Here are some basic moves:

  • Pawns move forward one square but capture diagonally.
  • Rooks move horizontally and vertically.
  • Knights move in an L-shape.
  • Bishops move diagonally.
  • Queens move horizontally, vertically, and diagonally.
  • Kings move one square in any direction.

Objective

The main objective of the game is to checkmate the opponent's king. This means the king is in a position to be captured (in check), and there is no legal move to remove it from capture.

Opening Principles

The opening phase is crucial. You should aim to control the center, develop your pieces (knights, bishops, rooks, etc.), and ensure the safety of your king.

Advanced Strategies

As you progress, you can explore advanced strategies like pins, forks, skewers, discovered attacks, and double attacks. Solving puzzles and practicing these concepts will enhance your gameplay.
